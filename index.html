About Me

I am a second-year Computer Science and Systems Engineering undergraduate at LIET, maintaining a 9.5 GPA, with a strong inclination toward building reliable, understandable software systems rather than surface-level applications.

My primary technical work revolves around Python-based backend development, academic systems, data processing pipelines, and structured problem solving. I enjoy working close to the logic layer — designing data models, workflows, and constraints that make software predictable and maintainable.

I am particularly interested in machine learning as a tool, not a buzzword — focusing on how models integrate with systems, how data flows are validated, and how results can be interpreted responsibly. Alongside this, I am developing a long-term interest in operating systems and compiler internals.

As Vice-Chairperson of the ACM Student Chapter, I work on creating technically meaningful environments — encouraging students to move beyond syntax toward understanding systems, trade-offs, and design decisions.

As Editor-in-Chief of the Lendi Write Club, I focus on clarity of thought and communication. Writing, for me, is not separate from engineering — it is how ideas are tested, refined, and made precise. Winning the District Poetry Competition (Yuva Utsav 2024) reaffirmed this balance between logic and expression.

I value mentoring and discussion. I believe understanding is proven not by knowing answers, but by being able to explain systems clearly, honestly, and without shortcuts.
