About Me

I’m a Computer Science Second Year student at LIET with a 9.5 GPA, passionate about Machine Learning and using technology to solve real-world problems. While I have solid foundations in Web Development, C, Python, HTML, and CSS.

As Vice-Chairperson of the ACM Student Chapter, I strive to foster a collaborative community where students can explore computing beyond the classroom. I am proud to help create opportunities—through talks, workshops, and projects—that empower peers to grow, connect, and innovate together.

As Editor-in-Chief of LWC, I work to communicate ideas clearly and meaningfully. I was humbly a proud member of the team that revived the Student Activity Centre (SAC), proving that steady, quiet effort can bring spaces back to life.

Writing keeps me grounded — winning the District Poetry Competition (Yuva Utsav 2024) reminds me that creativity and logic go hand in hand.

Mentoring friends and peers is important. As a great voice said, "If you couldn't explain it, You didn't understand it". I believe leadership is about listening, guiding, and helping others find their own path. I’m not aiming to have all the answers — just a willingness to grow and help others grow too.
